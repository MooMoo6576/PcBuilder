Learn the Parts

What Goes Inside a PC?

Every part plays a role. Here's what you need to know.

🧠

CPU Processor

The brain of your PC. It handles all instructions and calculations. More cores = better multitasking. Higher clock speed = faster single tasks like gaming.

Tip: AMD Ryzen and Intel Core are the two main brands to compare.
🎴

GPU Graphics Card

Renders everything you see on screen. Essential for gaming, video editing, and 3D work. The most important part for visual performance.

Tip: NVIDIA and AMD are the two main GPU brands. NVIDIA also offers AI-accelerated tasks.
🗂️

RAM Memory

Temporary workspace for your PC. More RAM lets you run more apps at once without slowdowns. 16GB is the modern standard; 32GB for creative work.

Tip: DDR5 is newer and faster, but DDR4 is still great value.
💾

Storage SSD / HDD

Where all your files, games, and OS live. SSDs are dramatically faster than old hard drives. An NVMe SSD is the best choice for your main drive.

Tip: Get at least 1TB SSD. Add a large HDD for extra storage if needed.
🔌

Motherboard

Connects all your parts together. Must be compatible with your CPU. Determines what RAM speeds and storage options you can use.

Tip: Match your motherboard to your CPU's socket type (e.g. AM5 for Ryzen, LGA1700 for Intel).
⚡

PSU Power Supply

Powers every component. Don't cheap out here — a bad PSU can damage your whole build. Look for 80+ Gold certification for efficiency.

Tip: Use a PSU calculator online to estimate wattage before buying.
🖥️

Case

Houses all your components. Airflow design matters for keeping things cool. Choose a size that fits your motherboard (ATX, Micro-ATX, Mini-ITX).

Tip: Mid-tower ATX cases offer the best balance of space and airflow.
❄️

Cooling

Keeps your CPU from overheating. Stock coolers are fine for basic use, but aftermarket air coolers or liquid coolers are better for performance builds.

Tip: For budget builds, a Noctua or DeepCool air cooler is excellent value.
Build Tips

Ready to Build? Here's What to Know.

Building a PC is easier than it looks. Follow these tips to avoid common mistakes.

01

Check Compatibility First

Before buying anything, verify your CPU, motherboard, and RAM all work together. Use a site like PCPartPicker to check compatibility automatically.

02

Don't Skimp on the PSU

A cheap power supply is one of the most common causes of PC damage. Stick to reputable brands and get a wattage rating with some headroom.

03

Ground Yourself

Static electricity can damage components. Touch a metal surface or wear an anti-static wrist strap while building to stay safe.

04

Install RAM Correctly

RAM slots have notches — only push in one direction. For dual-channel performance, check your motherboard manual for which slots to fill first.

05

Cable Management Matters

Good cable routing keeps airflow clear and makes your build look clean. Most modern cases have routing holes behind the motherboard tray.

06

Don't Force Anything

PC parts connect with gentle, firm pressure — nothing should require force. If something isn't fitting, stop and recheck alignment and orientation.
